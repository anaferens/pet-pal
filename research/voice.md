# PetPal — Voice

How PetPal speaks. Derived from [`CLAUDE.md`](../CLAUDE.md) (brief/tone), [`personas.md`](personas.md), [`jtbd.md`](jtbd.md), and [`research.md`](research.md) — including the [Competitor Language](research.md#competitor-language) audit. Every principle below is anchored to a real line; nothing is asserted on taste alone.

**Who we're talking to.** Primarily the **Organised Owner** (Eva/Ana) — the person who keeps the record and hands it on. Secondarily the **Receiving Caregiver** (sitter/walker/new vet) who opens a link with no account, and the **Worried Owner** in an out-of-hours scare.

**The line to hold.** The brief asks for two things that pull against each other: *"Warm, friendly — not clinical. Companion app, not medical record system"* **and** *"Trust and reliability (for the medical/legal side)"* ([CLAUDE.md → Design Direction](../CLAUDE.md)). We resolve the tension with one stance: **calm confidence — warm by default, precise when it matters.** Trust leads, because the owner is handing us the things they can't afford to lose.

> **Everything about your pet, in one trusted place.**
> *For every walk, vet visit, and weekend away.*

The headline carries the promise (**trusted**, not "complete"); the sub-line carries the mood (everyday, warm, landing on real moments). Wit and warmth are welcome in safe moments — onboarding, sharing, delight. They are switched off around health, money, legal, and emergencies.

---

## Principles

### 1 · Own "trusted," not "complete"

**Rule.** Every competitor promises *everything in one place*; we promise a place you can *rely on* — so we reassure that what's stored is safe, current, and yours, rather than boasting about how much it holds. Say it's saved, up to date, and shared only with people you chose; let "trusted" be the word we own.

**Example.**
- *Everything about Miso, in one trusted place.*
- *Saved. Only people you choose can see it.*
- Dossier subtitle: *Up to date — ready for the vet and the kennel.*

**Counterexample.**
- *The most complete pet care app.*
- *All your pet's info, in one place!*

**Rationale.** The category already saturates the completeness claim — "The most complete pet care app," "everything in between," "all in one place" ([research.md → Competitor Language #1–3](research.md#competitor-language)) — so it can't differentiate us. Meanwhile the felt need is *retrievable, provable* records: "simply stating your dog is up-to-date is usually not enough" at boarding/daycare/travel ([research.md §F9](research.md)), and records/proof is the **#1 adoption driver** ([§F10](research.md)). And the single most-repeated feeling in real reviews is *peace of mind* ([jtbd.md E1](jtbd.md)). Trust is both the unclaimed gap and the thing owners actually want.

---

### 2 · Warm by default, precise under pressure

**Rule.** Speak like a calm, organised friend — plain words, the pet's name, a light touch — and keep wit for the safe moments (setup, sharing, small wins). The instant the screen is about a vaccination, a payment, a legal document, or an emergency, drop the wit entirely and be exact and reassuring; the mood is our sub-line — *"for every walk, vet visit, and weekend away"* — everyday, but it lands on things that matter.

**Example.**
- *(warm)* *Nice to meet you, Miso.* · *That's the hard part done.*
- *(precise, same product)* *Rabies is due 15 Mar 2027. Kennels will ask for proof.*

**Counterexample.**
- *Oopsie! Something went wrong 🐾* — on a failed vaccination save.
- *Congratulations! 🎉* — after logging a medication.

**Rationale.** The brief wants a "companion app, not medical record system" **and** "trust and reliability for the medical/legal side" ([CLAUDE.md](../CLAUDE.md)). The market splits into cold ("A Professional Health Application for Your Pets," "medical/vet incidents") versus warm — and the warm ones win the tone: "your personal, digital health companion" (MyTherapy), "Kind help from real pet lovers" (Lassie), "Keep it next to yours" (Pet Health) ([research.md → Competitor Language #7,10,15,8](research.md#competitor-language)). But the Worried Owner's defining moment is a scare where PetPal serves "the information part, not the consultation" ([jtbd.md R5](jtbd.md)) — cheer there would be tone-deaf. Warm is the default; precise is non-negotiable when it counts.

---

### 3 · Speak to the moment, not the feature

**Rule.** Name what the owner is trying to *do* — prove Miso's jabs at the kennel, hand the sitter what they need — never the database behind it. Prefer everyday human labels over admin ones, and write empty states and buttons as the next real-world step, not as inventory management.

**Example.**
- Empty state: *Add Miso's first jab — kennels and groomers will ask for it.*
- Section: *Health & jabs* · *Who's caring for Miso* (not *Vaccination History* · *Access Management*).

**Counterexample.**
- *Manage your pet's medical records.*
- *Track flea & tick medication, medical/vet incidents, vaccinations.*

**Rationale.** Our own JTBD rule is "wording describes the person's progress, not the product — no feature names" ([jtbd.md](jtbd.md)), and the frequent felt need is a *moment* — proving vaccination at "boarding, daycare, grooming and travel" ([research.md §F9](research.md)) — not "medical records" in the abstract. The category defaults to feature-listing — "medical records, veterinary visits, unlimited pet seats" ([research.md → Competitor Language #4](research.md#competitor-language)) — while the one warm competitor names the *task*: Rover's **"care instructions,"** with the pet's names right in the empty state ([#16–17](research.md#competitor-language)). The sub-line *"for every walk, vet visit, and weekend away"* is this principle applied to the tagline.

---

### 4 · Make sharing feel safe and effortless

**Rule.** Around any handoff, always say *who* can see *what*, for *how long*, and that the recipient needs no account — turning the owner's guilt about leaving their pet into confidence that they've done it right. Name the boundary, not just the button.

**Example.**
- *Share Miso with a carer — read-only, expires in 1 month. No app or account needed for them.*
- *Only the people you choose. Revoke any time.*

**Counterexample.**
- *Share your pet!*
- A bare *Share* button with no scope, expiry, or "no account needed" anywhere in sight.

**Rationale.** The handoff is the high-emotion moment: **"56% of owners feel overwhelming guilt and anxiety leaving pets"** ([research.md §F11](research.md)), and the owner's job is "be an easy, trustworthy handoff" ([jtbd.md S2](jtbd.md)). No-signup access is the right default — gated "open" links are actively resented ([research.md §F7](research.md)) — and "share with a third party via a simple code/link" is the category's recurring **trust** mechanism ([research.md → Shared Patterns #2](research.md)), modelled best by MyTherapy's "safe and uncomplicated one-time code" ([Competitor Language #11](research.md#competitor-language)). Spelling out the scope is what converts anxiety into trust.
