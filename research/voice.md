# PetPal — Voice

How PetPal speaks. Derived from [`CLAUDE.md`](../CLAUDE.md) (brief/tone), [`personas.md`](personas.md), [`jtbd.md`](jtbd.md), and [`research.md`](research.md) — including the [Competitor Language](research.md#competitor-language) audit. Every principle below is anchored to a real line; nothing is asserted on taste alone.

**Who we're talking to.** Primarily the **Organised Owner** (Eva/Ana) — the person who keeps the record and hands it on. Secondarily the **Receiving Caregiver** (sitter/walker/new vet) who opens a link with no account, and the **Worried Owner** in an out-of-hours scare.

**The line to hold.** The brief asks for two things that pull against each other: *"Warm, friendly — not clinical. Companion app, not medical record system"* **and** *"Trust and reliability (for the medical/legal side)"* ([CLAUDE.md → Design Direction](../CLAUDE.md)). We resolve the tension with one stance: **calm confidence — warm by default, precise when it matters.** Trust leads, because the owner is handing us the things they can't afford to lose.

> **Everything about your pet, in one trusted place.**
> *For every walk, vet visit, and weekend away.*

The headline carries the promise (**trusted**, not "complete"); the sub-line carries the mood (everyday, warm, landing on real moments). Wit and warmth are welcome in safe moments — onboarding, sharing, delight. They are switched off around health, money, legal, and emergencies.

---

## Principles

### 1 · Own "trusted," not "complete"

**Rule.** Every competitor promises *everything in one place*; we promise a place you can *rely on* — so we reassure that what's stored is safe, current, and yours, rather than boasting about how much it holds. Say it's saved, up to date, and shared only with people you chose; let "trusted" be the word we own.

**Example.**
- *Everything about Miso, in one trusted place.*
- *Saved. Only people you choose can see it.*
- Dossier subtitle: *Up to date — ready for the vet and the kennel.*

**Counterexample.**
- *The most complete pet care app.*
- *All your pet's info, in one place!*

**Rationale.** The category already saturates the completeness claim — "The most complete pet care app," "everything in between," "all in one place" ([research.md → Competitor Language #1–3](research.md#competitor-language)) — so it can't differentiate us. Meanwhile the felt need is *retrievable, provable* records: "simply stating your dog is up-to-date is usually not enough" at boarding/daycare/travel ([research.md §F9](research.md)), and records/proof is the **#1 adoption driver** ([§F10](research.md)). And the single most-repeated feeling in real reviews is *peace of mind* ([jtbd.md E1](jtbd.md)). Trust is both the unclaimed gap and the thing owners actually want.

---

### 2 · Warm by default, precise under pressure

**Rule.** Speak like a calm, organised friend — plain words, the pet's name, a light touch — and keep wit for the safe moments (setup, sharing, small wins). The instant the screen is about a vaccination, a payment, a legal document, or an emergency, drop the wit entirely and be exact and reassuring; the mood is our sub-line — *"for every walk, vet visit, and weekend away"* — everyday, but it lands on things that matter.

**Example.**
- *(warm)* *Nice to meet you, Miso.* · *That's the hard part done.*
- *(precise, same product)* *Rabies is due 15 Mar 2027. Kennels will ask for proof.*

**Counterexample.**
- *Oopsie! Something went wrong 🐾* — on a failed vaccination save.
- *Congratulations! 🎉* — after logging a medication.

**Rationale.** The brief wants a "companion app, not medical record system" **and** "trust and reliability for the medical/legal side" ([CLAUDE.md](../CLAUDE.md)). The market splits into cold ("A Professional Health Application for Your Pets," "medical/vet incidents") versus warm — and the warm ones win the tone: "your personal, digital health companion" (MyTherapy), "Kind help from real pet lovers" (Lassie), "Keep it next to yours" (Pet Health) ([research.md → Competitor Language #7,10,15,8](research.md#competitor-language)). But the Worried Owner's defining moment is a scare where PetPal serves "the information part, not the consultation" ([jtbd.md R5](jtbd.md)) — cheer there would be tone-deaf. Warm is the default; precise is non-negotiable when it counts.

---

### 3 · Speak to the moment, not the feature

**Rule.** Name what the owner is trying to *do* — prove Miso's jabs at the kennel, hand the sitter what they need — never the database behind it. Prefer everyday human labels over admin ones, and write empty states and buttons as the next real-world step, not as inventory management.

**Example.**
- Empty state: *Add Miso's first jab — kennels and groomers will ask for it.*
- Section: *Health & jabs* · *Who's caring for Miso* (not *Vaccination History* · *Access Management*).

**Counterexample.**
- *Manage your pet's medical records.*
- *Track flea & tick medication, medical/vet incidents, vaccinations.*

**Rationale.** Our own JTBD rule is "wording describes the person's progress, not the product — no feature names" ([jtbd.md](jtbd.md)), and the frequent felt need is a *moment* — proving vaccination at "boarding, daycare, grooming and travel" ([research.md §F9](research.md)) — not "medical records" in the abstract. The category defaults to feature-listing — "medical records, veterinary visits, unlimited pet seats" ([research.md → Competitor Language #4](research.md#competitor-language)) — while the one warm competitor names the *task*: Rover's **"care instructions,"** with the pet's names right in the empty state ([#16–17](research.md#competitor-language)). The sub-line *"for every walk, vet visit, and weekend away"* is this principle applied to the tagline.

---

### 4 · Make sharing feel safe and effortless

**Rule.** Around any handoff, always say *who* can see *what*, for *how long*, and that the recipient needs no account — turning the owner's guilt about leaving their pet into confidence that they've done it right. Name the boundary, not just the button.

**Example.**
- *Share Miso with a carer — read-only, expires in 1 month. No app or account needed for them.*
- *Only the people you choose. Revoke any time.*

**Counterexample.**
- *Share your pet!*
- A bare *Share* button with no scope, expiry, or "no account needed" anywhere in sight.

**Rationale.** The handoff is the high-emotion moment: **"56% of owners feel overwhelming guilt and anxiety leaving pets"** ([research.md §F11](research.md)), and the owner's job is "be an easy, trustworthy handoff" ([jtbd.md S2](jtbd.md)). No-signup access is the right default — gated "open" links are actively resented ([research.md §F7](research.md)) — and "share with a third party via a simple code/link" is the category's recurring **trust** mechanism ([research.md → Shared Patterns #2](research.md)), modelled best by MyTherapy's "safe and uncomplicated one-time code" ([Competitor Language #11](research.md#competitor-language)). Spelling out the scope is what converts anxiety into trust.

## Glossary

**One concept, one term.** This table resolves every naming inconsistency flagged in [`microcopy.md §11-A`](microcopy.md). It supersedes what's currently on the screens — applying it back across the prototype is a later step, not part of this decision. Terms are chosen from words already in the product, personas, or research; nothing new is invented.

| Concept | ✅ We say | ✖ Never | Why (in plain terms) |
|---|---|---|---|
| The pet's whole record (the hub) | **pet card** · *Miso's card* | pet dossier, pet profile, "My pet" | Owners already picture a **card** — research calls the thing PetPal replaces "the crumbling paper card… vets and boarding facilities prefer" ([research.md §F1](research.md)). "Dossier" is a file-on-a-suspect; "profile" is a dating app. A card is what you keep in your wallet and hand over — warm, and true to the mental model. |
| The sitter's read-only view | **shared pet card** | Shared pet view, Miso's profile, shared profile, shared view | It *is* the pet card, shared and read-only — so it keeps the same name. Same object, one word; the sitter doesn't learn a second vocabulary. |
| A part of the pet card | **section** | block, tab, module, area | "Section" is already the only word used in product copy ([microcopy.md §9](microcopy.md)); we lock it so no one reaches for "block." |
| Personality & care content | a **section** that's *filled* / *empty* | "Profile filled", "the profile" | Personality & care is a **section of the card**, not a separate "profile." Status reads **"Filled · updated 5 days ago,"** not "Profile filled." |
| A shot the pet received | **jab** (everyday) · *vaccination* (proof/export only) | vaccine | The section is already **"Health & jabs"** — warm and plain, the word an owner uses at the kennel. "Vaccine" is the vial, not the record. The one allowed exception is a proof/export surface, where the precise legal word **"vaccination"** is correct — that's [Principle 2](#2--warm-by-default-precise-under-pressure) (precise under pressure), not a second term for everyday use. |
| Emergency permissions | **authorisation** · *emergency cover* | authorization (US spelling) | Resolves the British/American split in the inventory ([microcopy.md §11-A](microcopy.md)). The product is already British — *favourite*, *behaviour* — so we standardise the spelling, not toggle it mid-app. |
| Phoning a person | **Call** · *Call the vet* · *Call Eva* | Contact | The button literally dials (`tel:`). "Call" is what happens; "Contact" is an admin word for a thing that doesn't. This is [Principle 3](#3--speak-to-the-moment-not-the-feature) — name the action. |
| Making an account | **Create account** (pairs with **Log in**) | Sign up (as a button) | "Create account / Log in" describes the two actions plainly. "Sign up" as a button competes with "Create account" for the same tap — pick one. |
| An empty section | **No … yet** — *No documents yet* | "No insurance added", "No vet clinic saved" | One pattern. **"…yet"** is warm and forward-looking — it assumes you'll add it — where "added/saved" reads like a system report. |
| The account-holder | **owner** — addressed as **you** | user, account holder, member | The primary persona is literally the **Organised Owner** ([personas.md](personas.md)); the tab is "Owner." "User" is how software talks about people, not how a companion app does. |

### How we address the reader

Always the **informal, singular "you"** — the reader is one person, spoken to directly, on every screen. In German, this is **"du," never "Sie"**: the brief wants something "easy for 16-year-olds and 70-year-olds alike" ([CLAUDE.md → Key Design Values](../CLAUDE.md)), and a companion doesn't hold you at formal distance. Never the third person — no *"the user should…," "the owner can…," "pet owners are able to…"* Write *"Add Miso's details,"* not *"The owner adds the pet's details."*

### Spelling & loanwords

- **House spelling: British English.** *favourite, behaviour, authorise/authorisation, licence, cancelled* — the product already uses these, so we finish the job rather than mixing in US forms. This is the one call that closes the `authorisation/authorization` split.
- **Loanwords we keep** — the everyday words real owners (incl. our German market) actually say: **App, Link, PDF, Chip** (for microchip), **Sitter, Update, Vet**. These read as normal, not imported.
- **Loanwords we refuse** — corporate/product jargon that breaks the plain, warm register: **Dashboard, Onboarding, CTA, "Manage", "Access Management", "records management".** If it sounds like it belongs in a settings panel at work, it doesn't belong here.
- **Regulated terms stay exact:** *EU Pet Passport* and *microchip* follow the official naming ([CLAUDE.md → Compliance](../CLAUDE.md)) — trust beats warmth on the legal surfaces.

## Forbidden

**Language we never use.** Each row pairs an actual line from the [inventory](microcopy.md) (or the pattern the user called out) with the way it should read instead. The "instead" column follows the principles above.

| Never | ✖ Before (from the inventory) | ✅ Instead |
|---|---|---|
| **"Something went wrong"** (the AI-error cliché — the heading on **10** error screens) | *Something went wrong* | *We couldn't save Miso's record.* — then the specific reason + next step, which the bodies already do well ([microcopy.md §6](microcopy.md)). |
| **"Oops" / "Oopsie"** | *Oops, something went wrong 🐾* | *We couldn't reach the server.* Name what failed, plainly. An error is not a moment for a mascot. |
| **"Welcome" / "Welcome back"** | *Welcome back* (login heading) | *Log in* — the screen already knows what you're here to do; state the task, don't greet. |
| **"Congratulations!" / "Awesome!"** | *Awesome — all set up! 🎉* | *That's Miso's card ready. You can share it whenever you like.* — say what's done and what's next; the win is the fact, not the fanfare. |
| **Motivational / "journey" coaching** | *Start your pet journey by filling this info* | *Add Miso's details — you can change them any time.* We help you do a task; we don't narrate a personal-growth arc. |
| **Exclamation marks** (in system copy) | *Shared!* · *Done! This is what your sitter sees* | *Shared with Marie.* · *This is what your sitter sees.* Calm confidence doesn't shout. |
| **Emojis in system messages** | *Your pet's info was shared! 🎉* | *Miso's card is shared. Marie can open it — no account needed.* Warmth comes from the pet's name and the clear scope, not a party popper. |
| **"successfully"** | *Saved successfully.* | *Saved.* The word adds nothing; a plain "Saved." is calmer and reads as *more* certain, not less. |

*Emojis and wit aren't banned everywhere — they're fine in genuinely light moments (a first-run greeting, the personality section). They are banned in **system messages**: saves, errors, confirmations, anything touching health, money, legal, or emergencies. That boundary is [Principle 2](#2--warm-by-default-precise-under-pressure).*
