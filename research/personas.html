01 — Who

Personas

Three personas, one marked primary. Every claim is tagged by how well the research supports it; ? marks and hypothesis labels flag what is not yet evidenced.

The Organised Owner

"Eva, 34 — one dog, comfortable with apps" demographics [?]

★ Primary
Context

Treats her dog's admin like her own: checks when the dog was last vaccinated to plan the next one, and when she travels needs to hand a sitter everything important. One need underneath both — a single trustworthy record she can both consult and pass on. brief + indirect

Jobs
  • Look up health history and get reminded before the next thing is due. brief
  • Produce a "here's my dog" pack for a sitter without rewriting it each time. research · Rover
  • Share with a vet or sitter without making them create an account. research
Pains today
  • Info is scattered across notes apps and paper. Corrected: people do switch — but only past a tedium tipping point, and only if the new tool is low-friction. research · F2/F4
  • Sitter info lives in Google Sheets templates, PDFs, texted photos and WhatsApp — buried, no single "current state." research · F3
  • Facebook groups / asking acquaintances for a sitter. hypothesis searched, not confirmed ?
  • The handoff is emotionally loaded — 56% feel guilt & anxiety leaving their pet (intense, but episodic). research · F11
Trust triggers
  • Convinces: a record that stays current and shares instantly; low-friction. research
  • Puts off: having to re-enter everything — capture friction is the real make-or-break. research
  • A clinical / cold tone would put her off. hypothesis no user has said this ?
"It is possible to automatically create health reports in PDF format… you can send it by email… it can also help family members keep track." — Tom, MyTherapy review (real Trustpilot voice)

Why primary: her core job — keeping and proving pet records — is the most frequent felt need and the #1 reason people adopt pet apps (F9/F10). Rationale upgraded from the earlier, weaker "she exercises the full loop." The record/proof need recurs at every boarding, daycare, grooming and travel touchpoint.

The Worried-at-the-Wrong-Moment Owner

Facing a "something's not right" moment, often out of hours

Secondary
Context

The pet seems unwell, it's late, the clinic is closed — they need information or reassurance now, and reach for whatever is fastest. research · FirstVet

Jobs
  • Get fast access to the pet's info and to help when anxious. research
  • Pull up meds / conditions / vet contact in one tap. hypothesis ?
Pains today
  • Out-of-hours emergency vets are expensive and stressful for the animal. research
  • Scrolling chats / groups for "has anyone had this?" hypothesis ?
Trust triggers
  • Convinces: speed and a real, named professional. research
  • Puts off: expectation mismatch — finding out the tool can't do the urgent thing reads as a "con." research
"We had a video call with Dr Cato as our cat 'wasn't right'… I got a video call within half an hour… Turns out our cat has pancreatitis." — jess, FirstVet review (real Trustpilot voice)

Why secondary: the emotion is the most intense of the three jobs, but the moment is rare per person, and PetPal can serve only the information slice — not the consultation. Low frequency + partial serviceability. F11

The Receiving Caregiver

Sitter / walker / new vet — the person on the other end of the share

Secondary
Context

They've never met this animal and need to understand it fast: what it eats, what it's scared of, what it can't be near, who its vet is. The content model is proven by Rover; a caregiver info-checklist is documented across the industry. brief + research · F5

Jobs
  • Scan one screen and "get" the pet in seconds. research · Rover
  • Know what they're allowed to do in an emergency — vet authorization + a spend limit. New need, not yet in PetPal's model. research · F6
  • Send the owner reassuring updates back. hypothesis no sitter voice ?
Pains today
  • Corrected — no longer "all hypothesis": needs are documented (missing emergency contacts, no vet/authorization, scattered info), and a sitter "only sees what the owner entered," so a half-filled handoff leaves them flying blind. research · F5/F8
  • The caregiver's own lived voice is still missing. ?
Trust triggers
  • Convinces: helping without creating an account — supported by analogy (92% abandon rather than reset a password; gated forms convert ~2–3%). analogy · F7
  • Whether they'll use a link vs. fall back to "just text me" is untested. ?
"It is wonderful to know that you can be provided with pictures and updates from time to time." — Andre, Rover review · owner-side proxy — no caregiver quote exists in the research

Why secondary: follow-up research firmed up what this persona needs and the no-signup default, but it still has no first-person sitter/walker/vet voice. It remains the highest-risk persona — the clearest target for the next round of primary research.

02 — What they're trying to get done

Jobs hierarchy

Format: "When [situation], I want [motivation], so that [outcome]." Wording stays on the person's progress — no feature names. Unsupported jobs sit in the Hypotheses box, not the main list.

🎯 Main product job · Organised Owner

When I'm responsible for a pet's health and care over its whole life, I want to keep everything I know about it somewhere I trust and can pass on, so that I can act in time and never scramble to find or explain what matters.

🪜 Related jobs on the way — each tied to a persona & source
1

When a routine or preventive treatment is coming up, I want to know before it lapses, so that my pet stays protected and I'm not reacting last-minute.

Organised Owner · brief + research · table-stakes — already well covered by the market
2

When I'm leaving my pet with someone who's never cared for it, I want them to grasp what it needs in seconds, so that it's looked after the way it would be with me. MVP core

Organised Owner → Receiving Caregiver · research · Rover
3

When I'm dealing with a vet, I want to hand over my pet's real history without relying on my memory, so that they can help based on facts. MVP core

Organised Owner · research · MyTherapy
4

When I'm apart from my pet, I want reassurance that it's fine, so that the time away isn't spent worrying.

Organised Owner / Receiving Caregiver · research · Rover · ? no feature behind it yet
5

When something seems wrong and the clinic is closed, I want fast access to what I know and to help, so that I can decide well under stress.

Worried Owner · research · FirstVet · PetPal serves the information part only
❤️ Emotional jobs
E1

When I can't be with my pet, I want to feel calm instead of anxious, so that I'm not carrying low-level dread all day.

Organised / Worried Owner · research · "peace of mind"
E2

When I hand my pet to someone else, I want confidence nothing important was forgotten, so that I'm not second-guessing myself.

Organised Owner · partial framing is interpretation ?
E3

When my pet needs care, I want the experience to be as calm for it as possible, so that I'm not putting it through avoidable distress.

Organised Owner · research · Felmo · ? PetPal can't serve this directly — gap
👥 Social jobs
S1

When more than one person looks after my pet, I want us all working from the same picture, so that nothing falls through the gaps. MVP core

Organised Owner · research · MyTherapy
S2

When I ask someone to care for my pet, I want them to feel I've made it easy and complete, so that they take it seriously and help again.

Organised Owner → Receiving Caregiver · low-friction supported · "be seen as trustworthy" ?

🧪 Hypotheses — not supported by current data

  • Finding/choosing a carer in the first place. No data; PetPal does no matchmaking. ?
  • The carer's own job ("do it right, get rebooked"). Zero caregiver voice exists. ?
  • Escaping the messy status quo. Channels confirmed, but the pain intensity is unmeasured — people may be content with "good enough." ?
  • Jobs of non-dog/cat owners (rabbit, guinea pig, bird) — 3 of 5 target species, no voices. ?
  • How people decide to adopt a tool like this — "compare reviews" is real, but observed for a hardware purchase, not an app. ?
